CertCheck.me is a small web project explaining security certificates while browsing internet using https. It shows how to read a certificate and understands risks of using unverified ones. It also shows ways in which certificates can be manipulaed so that the manipulating side can collect private information being tranfered over https - such as logins, passwords and other sensitive data. [ read more ▾ ]

When we browse the internet using HTTPS, it means our connection is encrypted and safe for sending over it our personal data. To make sure that it is encrypted with the the authentic key of the website we visit, a Certificate Authority (CA) issue a certificate to the website owners. This certificate is a way for our browser to determine if the web site we are opening is the one we can trust and are indending to visit.

The problem is that certificates could be manipulated by tird parties to start MAN in the Middle (MITM) attacks: basically an attackers would impersonate the websites your are trying to connect to and intercept your data. We noticed that when browsers detect a manipulated certificate they do not explain to the users the risks associated in a clear way. That is why we invite you to play with this website and test your browser to see how it reacts to different kinds of certificates. We emulated here some «attack scenarios», but don't worry, they are completely harmless on this web site. They are only here to make you familiar with the reactions of your browser to various HTTPS related events.
